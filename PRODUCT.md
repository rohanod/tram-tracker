# Product

## Register

product

## Users

One private user saving tram vehicle numbers quickly, usually on a phone while near or inside a tram in Geneva. The primary job is to capture the vehicle number with the least possible friction, then correct the route leg if the automatic default is wrong.

## Product Purpose

Vehicle Tracker records a private history of tram vehicle observations. Success means the user can enter a 3-4 digit vehicle number, get a useful route-leg default from current location and time of day, save it locally and remotely, and still keep working when the installed app is offline.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, utilitarian, precise. The interface should feel like a single-purpose field tool, not a dashboard or a transit-themed landing page.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing pages, achievement systems, charts, decorative transit illustrations, dark control-room dashboards, and stats-first layouts. V1 is a saver, not an analytics app.

## Design Principles

- Save first: keep the primary path focused on vehicle number, route default, and save.
- Defaults are suggestions: automatic classification should be editable before and after save.
- Local trust: device storage is a first-class save target, not just a visual cache.
- Privacy by default: require the allowlisted Google account before exposing or syncing data.
- Utility over decoration: use restrained surfaces, clear controls, and compact review rows.
- Automation-friendly: support a token-protected shortcut API and URL prefill for location-based capture flows.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast and visible focus states. Keep controls large enough for mobile use, support reduced motion, and avoid color-only status communication.
